Project

The idea that I have thought of is a contact tracing Sherrin football. There will be a sensor that will log where on the ball it has made contact with the foot or hand. This will allow for people to gain an understanding of how the ball is being kicked and handballed. This could be beneficial for AFL players as it will show the average point made contact which could help each team improve their skill sets allowing them to be more competitive. It can also be used as a training method for new players who want to improve at the sport.

Motivation for this product was inspired by the Mark Williams Sherrin Football. This yellow football has a visible red spin line included to help kick the footy correctly which enhances the player to kick better. It is used by AFL clubs Richmond & Western bull dogs already which indicates its success. As someone who plays footy, I feel like this product would benefit my skills as a player. A Footy with a chip inside of it would give you even further feedback with a technology element enabling it to log even more precise.

The Sherrin pressure football is new in its class with spiderweb sensors. There are two main sensors on each side of the footy which is passed by static wire which picks up the contact on the outside of the football. This wire acts as a spiderweb running through the entirety of the inside leather. It will act as pressure sensor which will transmit a signal with the chip which is also located in the footy. From here the signal will be passed through to the Bluetooth module which can transmit the signal to the app. The Bluetooth technology will be using Bluetooth 5.0 which will allow the data to transfer up to speeds of 2 Mbps which is twice the speed of 4.2. Whilst also having faster download speeds 5.0 also supports extended range which at 243 meters up from 10 meters which is beneficial as it allows players to comfortably roam the ground without having to worry about connection issues. In order for this to work properly the materials used would have to obtain being leather. Leather is the only material that will work efficiently as it is strong flexible and durable enough to be a housing for these components, unlike a synthetic one due to its elasticity. The stitching of the football contains another layer running across the top to support the hardware on the inside. The football is available in red and yellow simulating the match balls to which will be red stitching on the yellow footballs and yellow stitching on the red footballs to differentiate a pressure football from a standard one. Available in size 5, 4, 3 and 2. The app is called “AFL Training” which is compatible on iPhone & android on the respected app stores. It can be downloaded for free and available to any user 4+. Each football comes with a linking code which is then inputted into the app. From here each time the user starts a session they can look at average contact points made represented by dots on a virtual football for that session. It will use machine learning to try and correct the individuals kick which will improve as the individual uses the app.

For this to work efficiently an app would be created which would require coding. This would enhance the experience of the user as it would display logs in a fancy way keeping them engaged with their training. The hardware required would be a match day football, sensors, a chip, which would include processing and Bluetooth. In order to make this work I would need to find someone with experience in app development to create the app. I would also need to work with a designer/engineer to come up with different concepts of how the hardware can be compatible with each other. Finally, a manufacturer which would be sourced in Melbourne by getting in contact with the Sherrin factory.

The outcome of this project will enhance skills for professionals and propel training for new players. There is no problem with the original although the help of a technology integrated football would be beneficial to the game. The outcome would also cause the players to improve professionally which may bring in more money and sponsorships for the game. It could also breach the gap for normal AFL & AFLW in terms of player salaries which has been a problem for the sport.
